  • High Concept!: Player must avoid the squares to survive.

  • Player Experience Goals: I want players to have a sense of escalating intensity, as the screen fills up with enemies over time.

  • Genre: This game would classify as a top-down bullet hell.

  • Game play: The player can use the left and right arrow keys to turn the green ship left and right. The up arrow key can be used to accelerate the ship forward.

    The enemies are spawned on the edge of the screen (randomly) every 4 seconds. If an enemy hits the player's ship, the player loses. The score is displayed in the top left, and it will increase constantly as the player survives the barrage of squares.

    In order to restart the game, the player must refresh the page.

  • Prototype goal: Basic top-down ship controls, player survival

  • Features: The player has no defense against the enemies, so they must constantly plan out safe paths through the enemies.

  • Setting/Premise: The game is abstract - the premise is just what it is: it's a game.

  • Story: N/A

  • Target Audience: People who like old arcade-style games. The style is meant to resemble asteroids.

  • Play time: Game could last anywhere from a few seconds to several minutes depending on how long the player can survive.

  • Strategies: The player must constantly be spotting safe spots and paths for the ship to survive. Generally, avoiding the center is a good idea once there are many enemies on screen.
